Welcome to the **Standards and Research in AI Benchmarking** repository.

### üåü Introduction

The rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AI) requires rigorous, standardized, and reproducible methods for performance evaluation. This repository serves as a central, community-driven resource to track, discuss, and disseminate critical information related to the **standards, research, and best practices** underpinning AI benchmarking.

Our goal is to foster a shared understanding of the methodologies used to assess AI capabilities across various domains, including machine learning models, hardware accelerators, and specific application areas (e.g., natural language processing, computer vision, robotics). ### üéØ What You Will Find Here

* **Standards Tracking:** Information and links related to active and proposed international standards (e.g., from IEEE, ISO/IEC, MLPerf, SPEC) that govern AI testing and benchmarking.
* **Research Summaries:** Curated lists and summaries of seminal or recent academic papers focusing on the **validity, fairness, reliability, and methodology** of AI performance metrics.
* **Methodology Deep Dives:** Resources that break down complex benchmarking techniques, such as adversarial robustness testing, data efficiency metrics, and energy consumption standards.
* **Tooling & Frameworks:** A non-exhaustive list of open-source tools and frameworks designed for automated AI performance measurement and comparison.

### üìù How to Contribute

Contributions can be made in several ways. Please adhere to the **Code of Conduct** (if you plan to add one) and follow the guidelines below:

#### 1. Suggest a New Resource or Standard

* **Goal:** To add a link, paper, or standard to the repository.
* **Action:**
    * **Open an Issue:** Create a new issue titled `[Resource Suggestion]: Name of Standard/Paper`.
    * **Provide Details:** Include a brief summary of the resource, a direct link, and specify which directory it belongs in (e.g., `standards/`).

#### 2. Submit an Improvement or Fix (Pull Request)

* **Goal:** To fix typos, update broken links, or add new, well-structured content (e.g., a new section for a standard).
* **Action:**
    1.  **Fork** this repository.
    2.  Create a descriptive **branch** (e.g., `feature/add-mlperf-summary`).
    3.  Place new files in the appropriate **directory** listed above.
    4.  **Commit** your changes with a clear message.
    5.  Open a **Pull Request (PR)** against the `main` branch.

### ‚úÖ Formatting Guidelines

* **Titles:** Use Markdown headings (`#`, `##`, `###`) appropriately within your submitted files.
* **External Links:** Always use descriptive link text.
* **File Naming:** Use all lowercase and hyphens as separators (e.g., `iso-iec-23053-summary.md`).
